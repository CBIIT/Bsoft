Calculates resolution measures.

Parameters
*pfirst image (typically a raw or experimental image).
*pmodsecond image (typically a model image).
*prealmaskreal space mask (can be fuzzy).
*pftmaskreciprocal space mask (0 & 1, indicating inclusion of structure factors).
hi_reshigh resolution limit for calculation.
aligned_reshigh resolution limit for validation.
sampling_ratioradial sampling ratio (1 for per voxel sampling).
Returns
Bplot* plot with 5 columns for each image. 
The two images must be of the same dimensions.
The two images are packed into a combined complex data set (the first
image in the real part and the second image in the imaginary part).
This is Fourier transformed and the individual transforms calculated
based on the Hermitian or Friedel symmetry of transforms of real space
data sets. The calculations are done on only half of the data set
because the two halves are Friedel symmetry related.

An optional input mask can be used to exclude parts of the transforms.
The mask must consist of 0's and 1's, where 1 indicates inclusion.

FRC: Fourier ring/shell correlation 
-----------------------------------
Saxton & Baumeister (1982) J. Microscopy 127, 127-138 
de la Fraga et al. (1995) Ultramicroscopy 60, 385-391 
This is also called Fourier shell correlation (FSC) in 3D. 
           sum(|F1|*|F2|*cos(phi1 - phi2)) 
FRC/FSC = --------------------------------- 
          sqrt( sum(|F1|^2) * sum(|F2|^2) ) 

DPR: Differential phase residual 
-------------------------------- 
Frank & Boublik (1981) Science 214, 1353-1355   
The resolution is determined by a sum of the phase differences weighed by the 
sum of the amplitudes: 
           sum( (|F1| + |F2|) * (phi1 - phi2)^2 ) 
DPR = sqrt(--------------------------------------) 
                      sum(|F1| + |F2|) 

SSNR: Spectral signal-to-noise ratio 
------------------------------------ 
Unser et al. (1987) Ultramicroscopy 23, 39-52 
The idea is that the signal-to-noise ratio is given by the model amplitudes  
divided by the noise.  The noise in the first data set is calculated by 
subtracting the second data set (the model).  The SNR is then estimated by 
subtracting one to compensate for residual noise in the model: 
         sum(|F2|^2) 
SSNR = --------------  -  1 
       sum(|F1-F2|^2) 

RAB: R(A+B) figure of merit 
--------------------------- 
Winkelmann et al. (1991) J. Cell Biology 114, 701-713 
The measure given in the paper has been adapted to divide by the sum of the 
amplitudes rather than just the amplitudes of the first data set, thus it 
becomes the difference divided by the sum: 
       sum(|F1 - F2|) 
RAB = ---------------- 
      sum(|F1| + |F2|) 

Individual structure factor figure of merit
-------------------------------------------
Each individual structure factor/reflection/voxel is assigned a FOM based
on the phase and amplitude differences, and a measure of the S/N ratio:
                          |A1 - A2|              A1 + A2
FOM = cos(delta_phi)*(1 - --------- )*(1 - exp(- ------- ) )
                           A1 + A2                StDev
where delta_phi is the phase difference, A1 and A2 are the amplitudes, and
StDev is the standard deviation of the combined transform (a measure of the
noise in the two data sets).

double* img_resolution_display ( Bplot *  plot)

Calculates the resolution estimates from a plot generated by img_resolution.

Parameters
*plotresolution plot information.
Returns
double* array of 4 resolution estimates. 
The plot must have 2 columns for each page as follows:
1.  spatial frequency
2.  FSC

The resolution estimates are calculated from the FSC curve at 4 cutoffs:
1.  0.143   gold standard (independent data sets)
2.  0.3     cutoff for halfsets (not necessarily independent halfsets)
3.  0.5     conservative cutoff
4.  0.9     cutoff for selecting a resolution limit for the next round of processing.
